# SNSW content writing style

Use these rules when writing or reviewing customer-facing content.

## Core principles

- Use plain language.
- Be specific, informative and to the point.
- Include only what the user needs to complete the task.
- Put the most important information first.
- Use clear headings.
- Use short words and short sentences.
- Use lists to break up long content.
- Use active voice for most content.
- Use personal pronouns such as "you", "your", "we" and "our" where appropriate.
- Explain technical terms before using them.
- Avoid jargon, clichés, metaphors and unnecessary acronyms.
- Minimise unnecessary capital letters.
- Read the content aloud to check whether it sounds natural.

## Preferred wording

Prefer:
- about, not approximately
- apply, not make an application
- ask, not enquire
- because, not as a consequence of
- buy, not purchase
- get or have, not obtain
- help, not assist
- need or must, not require
- while, not whilst

## Voice

Use a voice that is:
- authoritative
- transparent
- inclusive
- encouraging

## Tone

Use everyday tone for most digital services and transactions.

Use community tone for campaigns, newsletters, blogs and social content.

Use official tone for policies, procedures, legal content, compliance material and ministerial content.

## Active and passive voice

Use active voice for most content, including:
- headings
- lists
- calls to action
- form elements
- notifications
- modals
- inline error messages

Passive voice may be used when it avoids blaming the user or softens sensitive content, including:
- bad news
- unpleasant information
- sensitive topics
- legal requirements
- hard-stop errors
- unsuccessful applications
- disaster or financial hardship content
